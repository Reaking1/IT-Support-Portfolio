---

# 📝 Issue Summary

The user reported that both external monitors were no longer being detected when connected through a docking station. The laptop's built-in display continued to function normally.

The user mentioned that the primary monitor's DisplayPort cable had been unreliable for several weeks and suspected it had failed.

---

# 🎯 Objective

Restore dual-monitor functionality and identify the root cause of the display issue.

---

# 🔍 Troubleshooting Process

## Step 1 – Verify the Issue

Confirmed:

- Laptop powered on successfully.
- Internal display functioning normally.
- Both external monitors not detected.

---

## Step 2 – Inspect the Docking Station

Verified:

- Dock securely connected.
- Dock power adapter connected.
- No visible damage to the dock.

---

## Step 3 – Inspect Display Connections

Checked:

- DisplayPort cable connection.
- Monitor power cables.
- Dock video outputs.

No obvious physical damage observed.

---

## Step 4 – Test Windows Display Detection

Opened:

**Settings → System → Display**

Selected:

**Detect Displays**

Result:

No external displays detected.

---

## Step 5 – Isolate the Fault

Disconnected the suspected DisplayPort cable and replaced it with a known working cable.

Result:

- Primary monitor detected immediately.
- Secondary monitor also detected successfully.

Both monitors operated normally after replacing the cable.

---

# ✅ Resolution

The faulty DisplayPort cable was replaced with a known working cable.

Both external monitors were detected successfully through the docking station, restoring the user's dual-monitor setup.

---

# 📚 Root Cause

Failed DisplayPort cable preventing video output from the docking station.

---

# 💼 Business Impact

The user was unable to use a dual-monitor workspace, reducing productivity when working across multiple applications and spreadsheets.

Replacing the faulty cable restored the user's normal workflow.

---

# 💡 Lessons Learned

Although the user suspected a faulty DisplayPort cable, the issue was confirmed only after systematic troubleshooting. Replacing the cable with a known working one isolated the fault and resolved the issue. User observations can provide valuable clues, but they should always be verified before concluding the root cause.
